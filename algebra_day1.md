##Problem 13
The house Trevor's family lives in has \[6\] people (including Trevor) and 
\[3\] bathrooms. In the past month, each person showered for an average of 
\[480\] minutes and used an average \[72\] liters of shower water (over the entire month). Water costs \[0.20\] dollars per liter.
How much money did Trevor's family spend, in total, on shower water in the past month?

**Solution**
## Trevor Shower Water Problem

**Given**:
- $6$ people
- $72$ liters/person 
- $\$0.20$/liter

**Total water**:
$$6 \times 72 = 432 \text{ liters}$$

**Total cost**:
$$432 \times 0.20 = 86.40$$

**Answer**: $\$86.40$

##Problem 14
Two candles start burning at the same time. One candle is 
\[15\,\text{cm}\] tall and burns at a rate of 
\[5\,\text{cm}\] every 
\[6\] hours. The other candle is 
\[25\,\text{cm}\] tall and burns at a rate of 
 
\[2\dfrac{1}{2}\,\text{cm}\] every hour.
How tall will the candles be when they first burn down to the same height?

**Solution**
## Two Candles Problem

**Candle 1**: $15$ cm, burns $5$ cm every $6$ hours  
**Rate**: $\frac{5}{6} \approx 0.833$ cm/h

**Candle 2**: $25$ cm, burns $2\frac{1}{2} = 2.5$ cm/h

**Height equations**:
$$\begin{cases} h_1(t) = 15 - \frac{5}{6}t \\ h_2(t) = 25 - 2.5t \end{cases}$$

**Set equal**:
$$15 - \frac{5}{6}t = 25 - 2.5t$$

**Solve**:
$$\frac{5}{6}t - 2.5t = 15 - 25$$
$$-1.667t = -10$$
$$t = \frac{10}{1.667} = 6 \text{ hours}$$

**Height**:
$$h_1(6) = 15 - \frac{5}{6} \times 6 = 15 - 5 = 10 \text{ cm}$$

**Answer**: $$10 \text{ cm}$$

*Complete the recursive formula of \[h(n)\]*

**Solution**

3. Take \[(2)\]-\[\h(1)]: $-26-(-13)=-13$
   So the first term of the sequence is -13 and the common diference is -13
